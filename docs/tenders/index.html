Government Tenders

Every central government tender is published free. Almost no small supplier looks.

Publishing on the Central Public Procurement Portal is mandatory for every central ministry, department and public sector enterprise. It is free to search, open to anyone, and fed in real time by more than a hundred organisations. The barrier isn't access. It's that nobody told you it exists.

Where tenders actually live

There is no single national list. There are three systems, and which one matters depends on who is buying.

Central

CPPP — eprocure.gov.in

Run by NIC for the Ministry of Finance. Publication here is mandatory for all central ministries, departments, CPSEs and autonomous bodies. Real-time feed from 100+ organisations. Active, global, high-value and awarded tenders.

eprocure.gov.in →

Goods & services

GeM — gem.gov.in

Government e-Marketplace. Where departments buy off-the-shelf goods and services rather than run a full tender. If you sell products, this matters more to you than CPPP — the volume is enormous and the entry barrier is lower.

gem.gov.in →

State

GePNIC state portals

Every state runs its own instance of the same NIC software, on its own domain. State tenders do not appear on CPPP. You must go to your state's portal separately — this is the single most common reason people miss local work.

Using CPPP without getting lost

The portal is functional but not friendly. It was built for procurement officers, not for a first-time bidder.

Start with "Search Active Tenders"

Not the front page. Search by product category and state — organisation names won't help until you know who buys what you sell.

Read the closing date before anything else

Most tenders you find are already closing. Filter hard on the submission end date. If you have under a week and you've never bid before, it's usually not realistic — note the buyer and watch for their next one.

Check the EMD and the eligibility clauses first

Earnest Money Deposit, turnover thresholds, past-experience requirements. These are what actually disqualify you, and they're stated up front. Read them before the scope of work — it saves reading the whole document to discover you were never eligible.

MSMEs are exempt from EMD in many central tenders. See below.

You need a Digital Signature Certificate to bid

Not to search — to submit. A Class 3 DSC from a licensed Certifying Authority, in the name of the authorised signatory. Get it before you find a tender you want, not after; it takes days.

Register on the portal early

Enrolment is free but not instant. Do it while you're browsing, so the first tender that fits isn't lost to paperwork.

If you're a small business, register on Udyam first

This is the highest-value thing on this page, it's free, and it takes minutes.

Free · udyamregistration.gov.in

Udyam Registration

The government's MSME registration. Free, online, self-declared. You need Aadhaar and PAN. No GST required if your turnover is below the threshold — a common misconception that stops people registering.

udyamregistration.gov.in →

What it unlocks

Procurement benefits

Under the Public Procurement Policy for MSEs, central ministries and CPSEs have reserved procurement targets from micro and small enterprises, with sub-targets for SC/ST and women-owned enterprises. MSEs also commonly get EMD exemption and relaxed turnover/experience criteria.

Exact percentages and conditions change with policy — confirm the current position on the tender document and at msme.gov.in before relying on any figure, including ours.

Use only udyamregistration.gov.in. The Ministry's own portal says it plainly: "THIS IS THE ONLY GOVERNMENT PORTAL FOR MSME (UDYAM) REGISTRATION" and "Beware of Fake Sites. Udyam (MSME) Registration is Free of Cost and paperless." No agent, no fee, no private service is authorised to register you. A great many similar-looking sites charge for this. It is free.
Why we're pushing this. Most small suppliers who could bid never register, then assume tenders are for big companies. The reservation policy exists precisely to stop that being true. Registering costs nothing and changes which tenders you're eligible for. Over 9 crore enterprises have registered — the number of people who haven't is the whole reason this page exists.

Are you actually an MSME? The limits changed in 2025.

Revised with effect from 1 April 2025 — thresholds went up, so businesses that were too big before may qualify now. Check before assuming you don't.

Honest limits

State tenders are separate

CPPP is central only. Your state portal is a different site with a different login. There is no national list that includes both.

The anonymous view is limited

Without a logged-in session, CPPP's public widget shows only the latest handful of tenders and omits the issuing organisation. Register to see the full active list.

Beware paid "tender alert" services

Many resell what CPPP publishes free, often scraped in ways the portals prohibit. Some are useful for filtering. None have access you don't. Judge them on convenience, not on access, and never on claims of exclusivity.
